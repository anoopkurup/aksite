## CASE STUDY 1

### Marketing Agency

**From "performance marketers" to growth partners**

---

**The Symptom**

A marketing agency with a solid team and good results was stuck in a frustrating pattern. Every new client conversation turned into a price negotiation. Prospects would get proposals from three or four agencies and pick the cheapest one.

The founder assumed the problem was their sales process. Maybe they weren't selling hard enough. Maybe they needed better case studies. Maybe they should lower their prices to compete.

They tried improving their pitch decks. They tried offering discounts to close deals faster. Nothing changed — they kept losing to cheaper competitors.

---

**The Diagnosis**

**Layer 2: Positioning**

The agency was describing what they did — "performance marketing," "paid ads management," "digital campaigns." So was every other agency. Prospects couldn't see any difference, so they compared on the only metric they could: price.

The problem wasn't the sales process. It was that there was nothing distinctive to sell.

---

**The Fix**

We stopped talking about what they did and started talking about who they helped and what outcome they delivered.

The agency had a pattern they hadn't noticed: their best clients were all offline apparel brands going online for the first time. They understood this transition deeply — the inventory challenges, the brand positioning issues, the customer acquisition economics.

We repositioned them as **"growth partners for offline apparel brands going online."**

Not performance marketers. Not a general digital agency. A specific partner for a specific transition.

---

**The Result**

The price conversations stopped. Prospects who fit the positioning immediately saw why this agency was different — they understood the specific problem.

The agency moved from project-based fees to revenue-share deals with larger clients. They could charge more because they weren't competing on execution anymore — they were competing on expertise in a specific transformation.

**Layer fixed:** Positioning

**Time to clarity:** 4 weeks

## CASE STUDY 2

### Public Speaking Trainer

**From generic webinars to corporate training contracts**

---

**The Symptom**

An experienced public speaking trainer was running free webinars every month. Good attendance — 50 to 100 people would show up. Decent engagement during the session. But almost nobody converted to paid training.

The founder assumed the problem was the webinar itself. Maybe the content wasn't compelling enough. Maybe the call-to-action wasn't clear. Maybe they needed a better follow-up sequence.

They tried different webinar formats. They tried adding bonuses. They tried more aggressive follow-up emails. Conversion stayed flat.

---

**The Diagnosis**

**Layer 1: Strategy**

The webinars were attracting "anyone who wants to speak better" — a mix of students, job seekers, hobbyists, and professionals. Most of them had no budget and no urgent need. They attended because it was free and interesting, not because they were ready to buy.

The problem wasn't the webinar. It was who the webinar was attracting.

---

**The Fix**

We narrowed the target market from "anyone who wants to speak better" to **"mid-level managers in corporate India who need to present to senior leadership."**

This audience has budget (corporate training funds), urgency (career progression depends on visibility), and a specific problem (high-stakes presentations to executives).

The webinar content shifted from general public speaking tips to "How to present to the C-suite without losing the room." Same expertise, focused application.

---

**The Result**

Webinar attendance dropped — from 80 people to 30. But the 30 were the right 30. Conversion to paid training increased significantly.

More importantly, the trainer started getting inbound inquiries from HR departments looking for corporate training programs. The positioning made them findable for the right problem.

The trainer now commands premium pricing for corporate workshops. The free webinar became a lead generation tool that actually generates leads.

**Layer fixed:** Strategy

**Time to clarity:** 3 weeks

## CASE STUDY 3

### School Group

**From scattered marketing to location-centric campaigns**

---

**The Symptom**

A group running three schools in different parts of Bangalore was struggling with nursery admissions. Despite active marketing — social media, local ads, parent referral programs — admissions were stuck at around 70% capacity.

The management assumed they needed more marketing. More visibility. More reach. They were considering hiring an agency to "scale up" their campaigns.

But more of the same wasn't working. Each school had a different problem, and the one-size-fits-all approach was missing all of them.

---

**The Diagnosis**

**Layer 1: Strategy**

Three schools. Three different locations. Three completely different parent demographics.

One school was in an area with young IT professionals — dual-income families who valued modern pedagogy and flexibility. Another was in a more traditional neighbourhood where parents prioritised academic rigour and discipline. The third was in a developing area where affordability and safety were the primary concerns.

The marketing treated them as one brand with one message. It resonated with nobody in particular.

---

**The Fix**

We created **location-centric positioning** for each school.

Same brand, but different messaging based on what parents in each catchment area actually cared about. Different ad copy. Different parent testimonials featured. Different aspects of the school highlighted.

The IT-area school led with "learning through exploration" and flexible timings. The traditional-neighbourhood school led with "strong academic foundation" and experienced teachers. The developing-area school led with "safe, nurturing environment" and accessible fees.

---

**The Result**

This engagement is ongoing, but early results show improved response rates to localised campaigns. Parents in each area see messaging that speaks to their specific concerns, not generic "good school" claims.

The insight extended beyond marketing — it's now informing how each school positions itself in parent meetings, open houses, and even how teachers talk about the school's approach.

**Layer fixed:** Strategy

**Status:** Ongoing engagement

## THE PATTERN

### What these cases have in common

**1. The presenting problem was never the real problem.**

The agency thought they had a sales problem. The trainer thought they had a webinar problem. The schools thought they had a reach problem. None of them did.

**2. The fix was always foundational, not tactical.**

We didn't optimise their ads or rewrite their emails or build a new funnel. We fixed Strategy or Positioning — and then the tactics started working.

**3. Clarity created leverage.**

Once the foundation was solid, everything else got easier. Sales conversations. Content creation. Pricing decisions. The clarity compounded.
